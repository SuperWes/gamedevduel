Achievements

Achievements are mini-tasks or goals that you can target while developing your game for extra bragging rights. Making a game is your goal here, and going for achievements is not required, but we hope that going for achievements will help you follow some best practices and get involved with the community at a deeper level. There are a total of 50 achievements that will be rolled out in increments of 10 on July 8th, 15th, and 22nd.

To log achievements, please post your team name and the name of the achievement at http://stlgamedev.slack.com/messages/gdd_achievements/. If you need an account, contact us through this form with your name, email address, and team name.

Achievement Description Phase
Freebie Sign into slack and post a team name in the #gdd_achievements channel (https://stlgamedev.slack.com/messages/gdd_achievements) First Week
Movement Make something happen when you interact with the game First Week
Artsy Add at least one art asset into the game First Week
Slacker Create a private slack channel and invite your team to coordinate game progress First Week
Tracker Set up a Task Tracking board (https://trello.com/ or the tool of your choice) to prioritize tasks and track progress First Week
Check-in #1 Meet up with your team in person between June 30th and July 8th. We recommend the check-in event at Riot Games on July 6th (featuring Butterscotch Shenanigans) First Week
Git it on Add your game to version control (github.com, bitbucket.com, Subversion) First Week
Hardcore #1 Everyone on the team worked on the game for over 3 hours in one sitting between June 30th and July 8th First Week
Weekender #1 At least one person on the team spent at least 8 hours in total over the weekend of July 1st to July 3rd working on the game First Week
Game Loop You have the core gameplay loop working in your game First Week
Named Finalize the name of your game so that you can begin marketing it Second Week
StandUp Post a Daily Standup in the general Slack channel. In a Standup, mention what you did yesterday, and what you plan to do today. Standups are a great way to keep you focused on your goals! Second Week
Screenshot Saturday #1 July 9th Screenshot Saturday! Post a screenshot to Twitter with the name of your game and the hashtags #screenshotsaturday and #gamedevduel2016 and/or the Game Dev Co-op Facebook page (http://facebook.com/stlgamedev) with the hashtag #screenshotsaturday Second Week
Publisher Build an itch.io page for your game under our Game Jam (https://itch.io/jam/game-dev-duel-2016) Second Week
Songster Have at least one song in your game Second Week
Check-in #2 Meet up with your team in person to evaluate progress and discuss plans betweeen July 8th and July 15th. If your whole team is over 21, meet up at the check-in on July 14th at Orbit Pinball Lounge. Second Week
Death Your game has a loss state (free achievement if this is not applicable) Second Week
Winner Your game has a level or sub-section complete state working (free achievement if this is not applicable) Second Week
Hardcore #2 Everyone on the team worked on the game for over 3 hours in one sitting between July 8th and July 15th Second Week
Weekender #2 At least one person on the team spent at least 8 hours over the weekend of July 8th to July 10th working on the game Second Week
Diverse If your game has characters in it, make sure at least one of them is not a straight white person (free if your game has no characters) Ongoing
Open Source Your game is open source and the code is available in a public source control repository such as github.com or bitbucket.com (be sure to include a link when requesting the achievement) Ongoing
The Internet Your game contains a picture, gif, or video of a cat Ongoing
Committed Someone on your team has made at least one commit to source control on every day in July Ongoing
Multiple interpretations Your game unquestionably demonstrates more than one interpretation of the Game Jam theme Ongoing
Accessible Your game can be played effectively by someone who is either blind or has no limbs Ongoing
Character Development A main character in your game changes over the course of the game (free if your game has no characters) Ongoing
Shared Assets Your game features an asset or character from some other gamedevduel team. (coordinate through Slack) Ongoing
Orchestrated Your game features at least three songs that were written specifically for your game Ongoing
Archie Put a reference to or asset of the St. Louis arch somewhere in your game Ongoing
